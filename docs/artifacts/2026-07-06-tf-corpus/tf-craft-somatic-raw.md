## META / CRAFT ESSAY

### "The Tail Tale" — Bryan (Shifti) — https://shifti.org/wiki/The_Tail_Tale
A meta-essay on writing furry TF fiction, NOT a story. Core craft claims:
- "the more you say about it, the worse it's likely to come across"
- Recommends beginning "weeks, months, or better yet *years* after the date of the Big Change"
- Warns against the clichéd "Look! I'm a tiger!" story — transformation as spectacle
  without characterization/theme.
- Philosophy: render *aftermath* (psychology, daily friction, identity over time), not the
  mechanical change sequence. (An anti-pattern datapoint: under-showing is also a failure
  mode; the craft is selective sensory rendering, not exhaustive play-by-play.)

## SPECIES TF

### "Tale of a Tail" — Michael Bard (Shifti) — tail growth, first person
- "It felt weird at first, like something was shoving its way into my spine."
- "And it hurt like the dickens."
- "I felt pins and needles all down my back, and then my spine, well not my spine, but my
  kinesthetic awareness, stretched, outward and backward."
- "I felt bone and skin I'd never felt before, and long silky hairs growing out of it"
- "Cocking my head, I pulled muscles I'd never had before, and _looked_ at the long black
  hairy thing sticking out of my spine."
- "I yanked it away from him with my new muscles."
- "Then I relaxed and watched it wag back and forth."
- "its hairs brushing against my calves as it moved back and forth"

### "Off The Trail" (Shifti) — human→horse, third person
- "He felt a sudden wrench in his guts. He let out a strangled gasp of surprise"
- "bones aching and skin tingling with a weird sensation like intangible fingers being run
  through his body"
- "His shoes felt like they were shortening, crushing his toes, though he knew that in
  actuality it was probably his feet that were getting longer"
- "his shirt and pants already constricting oddly and bursting seams"
- "his balance was hopelessly off, he couldn't stand up straight"
- "He found it easier than attempting to stand, and getting easier all the time" (drift toward
  quadruped — new body's ease becomes the tell)
- "His heels ground painfully on the hard floor as he put weight on them"
- "With a whinnying scream of fear and surprise, John fell heavily against a stack of empty
  cardboard boxes" (voice changes mid-cry — autonomic + involuntary)
- "his vision blurred with tears"
- "pain suddenly stabbing through his feet"

### "Marduk's Gift" — Michael Bard (Shifti) — human→horse
- "hot energies pulsed through him"
- "His body rippled as muscle grew, and bones changed"
- "his vision blurred and changed"
- "his nostrils widened and grew in sensitivity"
- Post-change, sensation via object interaction: "The metal was cold and bitter, yet worn and
  loved"; "The pull at the reins yanked the rough-formed metal against his sensitive lips"
  (new anatomy KNOWN through friction with the world, not described directly)

### "Doppelganger" (Shifti) — horse→human reversal
- "Suddenly, Ivan let out an ear-splitting scream and he began to shake violently."
- "Fascinated, Sergei watched as the hooves softened, becoming pliable....like fingers!"
  (texture-state verb: softened/pliable; observer POV renders change)
- "His head was against the ceiling of the stable...With each crunch, Ivan's head pulled away
  from the ceiling. He was shrinking." (SIZE via ceiling gap — pure indirection; "crunch" as
  the audible increment)
- "Ivan collapsed on the floor of the stable, shaking violently. He took on a fetal position
  as his tail withered away."

## TG / GENDER TF

### "Métamorphie" — Forestier (Shifti, French) — M2F
- "Un sentiment de claustrophobie s'imposa aux deux voyageurs, mais ce fut bref. Il fut suivi
  par un sentiment de désorientation, puis un haut-le-cœur" (claustrophobia → disorientation →
  nausea — a *sequenced* interoceptive cascade)
- On waking female: "sa vessie réclamait son attention, mais quelque chose n'allait pas. Et
  ce tiraillement sur sa poitrine!" (bladder urgency + a *pull/tug* at the chest — new anatomy
  announced by an unfamiliar tension, not by naming breasts)
- "se diriger d'un pas incertain vers le miroir, toucher son visage imberbe, puis abaisser ses
  mains vers ses seins" (uncertain gait + exploratory self-touch — involuntary inventory)
- Height loss via relation: "j'avais rapetissé. J'était maintenant plus petite que Nati, ce
  qui signifiait que j'avais bien perdu 15 cm." (shrink registered by being now-shorter-than
  another person before any number)

## SIZE / EXPANSION / GROWTH

### "Towering Tonic" — Wattpad giantess growth story, part 5
- Growth through clothing failure: "bouncing and stretching her shirt and bra more and more as
  they continued...Myah's chest continued to grow, tightening her shirt until finally it tore
  right down the middle."
- "her rump still expanding and swelling inside them, it was a losing fight for those small
  black shorts. Rip!" (the garment as antagonist; "Rip!" as the discrete growth beat)
- Perspective/proprioception: "Amélie was now eye-level with her upper belly...She could hardly
  see the little french girl under her curvy bust." (scale shown by where another person's eyes
  now land, and by occlusion)
- "their breasts expanded and collided into each other...swelling warmly in her hands"
  (warmth + collision-with-environment as the felt register of expansion)
