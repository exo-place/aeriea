## CRAFT ESSAYS (technique stated directly)

### Shifti — "Transformation Writing Types"
- Physical TF: "concentrate on the physical sensations of the new form. What does it feel
  like to have antlers?"
- Psychological TF: "concentrate on dealing with the requirements of the new form, of
  experiencing the effects of the new body." (Noted as most common.)
- Poul Anderson principle: "appeal to at least three of the senses on every page."
- Anti-monotony via sensory range: "The writer has a huge toolbox of physical sensations,
  of touch and taste to use, that is rarely or with difficulty used in more common fiction."
- Sample detail: "the distant coldness of the needles on the forest floor beneath the
  tough pads of a wolf's feet." (concrete tactile, not "his feet changed")

### DeviantArt CrazyNaut — "How to Write a Transformation Story"
- Sequencing: "Go outwards from the first spot (hands to arms, hair to eyes, etc.).
  Transform a part that corresponds with the last in some way (hands to feet, stomach to
  chest, etc.)." — i.e. spatial logic / correspondence, not random order.
- Detail imperative: "DETAIL DETAIL DETAIL... if you use something as basic as 'his hands
  turned into dog paws', you might as well just write 'and then he became a dog', because
  your story has been rendered boring."
- Interest via faculties: "use their emotions, the five senses… things like that."
- Indirection: "just because something might be obvious, that doesn't mean you should make
  it obvious for the characters and apparent narrator."
- Weak vs strong example — moves from external announcement to internal realization:
  WEAK: "There was electricity at his hand, and he was growing yellow fur!... The changes
  were complete."
  STRONG: "He felt an electric tingling at his left hand... Once he thought the changes
  were finished, he looked down... once he saw his face in the mirror, he came to the
  realization." (delay the naming; land on interior realization)

## SCENES

### Shifti — "The Werehorse Chronicles" (NEGATIVE EXEMPLAR — the failure mode itself)
"My face was changing. My mouth was forming into a horse's muzzle."
"The rest of my body was also changing fast. My hair was now a horse's mane. My back
arched, my torso bent and expanded."
"In horror, I looked at my hands. My fingers were fusing into horse hooves. My feet were
changing."
"My back bent over and I was soon on all fours. I had turned into a horse!"
→ Note: monotone subject ("My X..."), flat "was changing/was Xing" verb, list cadence.
  This IS "X grows. X grows." with body parts swapped. Bar-to-clear negative sample.

### Shifti — "Mermaid Too" (duration framed, but change mostly off-page)
- Frames duration: "six weeks, forty two days, a gradual change" — then renders it via
  discrete observed states, not moment-by-moment.
- State-snapshot rendering: "Rather than wearing boots, I now had boot shaped feet... The
  merge at the top of the boot with the skin of my leg was seamless."
- Compression of a whole person-change into one clause: "Robert morphed in front of our
  eyes from man with tail to buxom, tightly waisted, long haired woman with tail."
→ Technique: long TF told as sampled STATES + one compressed montage clause, not a live tick.

### Wattpad — "Towering Tonic" pt5 (mid-craft growth; working moves visible)
"Her body slowly inched taller." / "Her breasts swelled larger and larger, bouncing and
stretching her shirt and bra more and more as they continued."
"Rip! Those shorts were history as Myah's ass sprung and bounced in the open air, still
swelling eagerly. When she finally came to a stop, Myah stood at 9'8"."
→ Techniques present: (a) clothing destruction as external PROXY for growth (measure the
  change by its consequence, not by restating "she grew"); (b) onomatopoeia "Rip!" as a
  beat/punctuation; (c) a terminal MEASUREMENT ("9'8"") to close the sequence — the "stop."
→ Weakness: leans on "swell/grow" repetition; sensation stays generic (moan/arch).

### DeviantArt KnightofGrowth — "Cassie Lang" (growth; the clearest anti-repetition working set)
Incremental w/ comparative sense: "First, she reached 2 meters tall, then 3 meters. It felt
different from shrinking — the world around her seemed to shrink gradually."
Numerical leaps (accelerate by widening the interval): "She passed twenty meters, then
forty, and continued to climb..."; later "past 100 meters... past 150 meters... past 200."
Focus-shift at scale: as size grows, description LEAVES the body and moves to environmental
impact / destruction rather than restating size.
Pairing: "the act of growing brought with it a sense of euphoria" — each beat carries a
psychological charge, not just a number.
→ Extractor's own summary of the technique: "Rather than describing each growth increment
  identically, the text varies its approach — using environmental impact, character
  responses, and numerical leaps to maintain pacing."
